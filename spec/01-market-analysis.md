### Resume Screening Segment

Resume screening and matching represents the largest use case within AI hiring:

- **25.8%** of AI hiring software usage is for resume screening/matching
- Recruiters rely most heavily on AI for initial candidate selection
- Companies report **50% reduction** in time-to-hire with AI screening
- **30% decrease** in cost-per-hire after implementing AI screening systems

**Source:** [The Interview Guys - AI Resume Screening Statistics](https://blog.theinterviewguys.com/83-of-companies-will-use-ai-resume-screening-by-2025-despite-67-acknowledging-bias-concerns/)

Organizations implementing AI resume screening report significant improvements:

| Metric | Improvement |

## Target Market

### Primary: Small-Medium Staffing Agencies

**Market Size:**
- **15,000+** staffing agencies in the United States
- **$150 billion** total staffing industry revenue (2024)
- Average agency size: 8-25 employees
- Average profit margin: 15-25%

**Buyer Persona: Agency Owner/Director**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Age** | 35-55 years old |
| **Title** | Owner, Managing Director, VP of Operations |
| **Company Size** | 5-50 employees, $2M-20M annual revenue |
| **Pain Points** | Manual screening bottleneck, recruiter burnout, missed placements |
| **Budget Authority** | Yes (can approve $150-500/month tools) |
| **Tech Savvy** | Medium (uses ATS but not highly technical) |
| **Channels** | LinkedIn, industry conferences, word-of-mouth |

**Industry Verticals (Initial Focus):**

1. **Healthcare Staffing** (30% of focus)
   - Nurses, CNAs, medical assistants
   - High volume (500-1000 resumes/week)
   - Standardized qualifications (licenses, certifications)
   - Urgency (hospitals need staff immediately)

2. **IT Staffing** (30% of focus)
   - Software developers, IT support, cybersecurity
   - Technical skills matching is critical
   - Higher margins ($50-100K placements)
   - Resume complexity (many technologies to evaluate)

3. **Warehouse/Logistics** (25% of focus)
   - Forklift operators, warehouse workers, drivers
   - Extremely high volume (1000+ resumes/week for large facilities)
   - Quick turnaround needed (48-hour hiring cycles)
   - Lower margins but high volume

4. **General/Mixed** (15% of focus)
   - Agencies covering multiple verticals
   - Administrative, finance, marketing roles

### Secondary: In-House Recruiting Teams

- **300,000+** companies with dedicated recruiters in the US

**Buyer Persona: Head of Talent Acquisition**

| Attribute | Details |

## Market Trends & Drivers

### 1. Remote Work Expansion

**Impact:** 300-500% increase in candidate volumes
- Pre-2020: Average 50-75 applications per job posting
- 2025: Average 250-400 applications per remote-eligible role
- Geographic barriers removed = more applicants to screen

### 2. Talent Shortage & Speed-to-Hire Pressure

**Impact:** First to screen wins the candidate
- 68% of candidates accept the first offer received
- Average time-to-offer: 24-36 days (2024)
- Companies screening in <7 days have 2.3x better acceptance rates

### 3. Bias & Compliance Concerns

**Impact:** Demand for transparent, auditable AI
- 67% of companies acknowledge bias concerns in AI screening
- Growing regulatory pressure (NYC LL 144, EU AI Act)
- Need for "explainable AI" and human-in-loop systems

**Our Positioning:** Screening assist tool, not auto-reject. Human makes final decisions.

### 4. Cost Pressures in Staffing Industry

**Impact:** Margins under pressure, need efficiency
- Staffing industry profit margins: 15-25% (down from 20-30% in 2019)
- Increased competition from gig platforms
- Need to do "more with less" (lean recruiting teams)

### 5. AI Democratization

**Impact:** SMBs now expect enterprise-grade AI
- OpenAI, Anthropic, etc. make AI accessible
- SMBs no longer accept "enterprise only" for AI tools
- Willingness to adopt if priced fairly
